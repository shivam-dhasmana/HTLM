Global warming refers to the long-term increase in Earth's average surface temperature due to human activities, primarily the emission of greenhouse gases such as carbon dioxide and methane. These gases trap heat in the atmosphere, leading to a rise in temperatures across the globe . As a result, glaciers are melting, sea levels are rising, and extreme weather events like heatwaves, droughts, and floods are becoming more frequent and intense. The burning of fossil fuels, deforestati clear on, and industrial pollution are major contributors to this crisis. If not addressed, global warming poses a serious threat to ecosystems, wildlife, and human life. Urgent action through sustainable practices, renewable energy, and international cooperation is essential to combat its effects and protect the planet for future generations.
